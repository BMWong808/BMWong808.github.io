Bath Wackathon 2024

24 hour Hackathon for Women and Gender Minorities

16th - 17th March

Signups are closed

If you have any queries regarding the event, please contact us via email at wit@bath.ac.uk


Welcome

Join us for the Bath Wackathon 2024! An in-person hackathon at the University of Bath for women and gender minorities.

You will be challenged to develop an innovative solution for a social issue in just 24 hours. You'll work in teams of up to four and have the chance to win some fantastic prizes through our tracks.

Thanks to support from our amazing industry partners, there will be an opportunity for all participants to develop their technical skills while widening their professional network. Our sponsors have further opportunities to fund tracks, provide goodies, judge categories and give prizes. All while meeting talented women and gender minorities who want to pursue careers in tech.

Being a woman or gender minority in technology is not always easy. With so few around, we are excited for an event designed for everyone to meet the other brilliant members of our community.




Theme

We are excited to announce the theme of this year's Bath Wackathon: Innovate for Social Good.

Teams are free to develop any project that aspires to make the world better in some way. This could be anything from an app to track your carbon footprint, to a wearable device designed to monitor stress levels.

Do not worry if you don't have an idea yet. Once announced, our technical and socially-themed tracks may give you some inspiration...

    FAQs

    In short, it is the ultimate technological group work task. You have 24hrs and a team to plan, design, develop and pitch a system from scratch. Whether you sacrifice functionality for ambition or strive for a fully working system the options are boundless. Pick a prize track and creatively solve the problem and you'll be real competition. As cliche as it sounds, the most important thing is have fun and meet new people.

    A prize track is a problem that needs to be solved or a question that needs to be answered. A specific goal your solution should fulfil

    Tracks can be vague or specific and your project can be eligible for more than one.

    Some tracks may be funded by our industry partners, so they will specially will be looking out for your solutions there.

    If you or your company have a question you want to see solved or want to see students achieve in your domain please contact th WiT committee at wit@bath.ac.uk and we will be happy to discuss more.

    Any woman or non-binary student of a university in the UK is able to participate.

    You don't have to be an expert in technology, whether you study a Computer Science masters or a linguistics foundation year, any women or non-binary student is welcome.

    No prior tech experience is needed. Just some love for creating solutions and wanting to network with our community.

    If you are a University of Bath student, our sibling student group, Bath Computer Science Society, are running Bath Hack on the 13th-14th April in person for all genders.

    While event sponsors have not yet been announced, we are excited to be working with some wonderful companies and institutions.

    If you or your company are also interested in supporting this event, please email WiT at wit@bath.ac.uk and we will be happy to discuss options.

    This event will be taking place in the University of Bath Campus in the gorgeous city of Bath

    We cannot guarantee to cover transportation costs or accommodation for participants, but we do recommend coming and enjoying this beautiful city (a UNESCO World Heritage Centre)

    You don't have to use the full time and we encourage you to take a break and/or sleep. But yes, 24hrs of potential project time.

    Good luck, use it wisely!

    About Us

    WiT Bath is a Women and Gender Minority run group within the Faculty of Science of The University of Bath and also a chapter of ACM-W. Our mission is to form an empowered community of mixed genders to encourage inclusion and diversity within the department and wider circles.

    The Asociation for Computing Machinery is the world's largest educational and scientific computing society. It delivers resources that advance computing as a science and a profession. ACM provides the computing field's premier Digital Library and serves its members and the computing profession with leading-edge publications, conferences, and career resources.

    ACM Women advocates internationally for the full engagement of women in all aspects of the computing field, providing a wide range of programs and services to ACM members and working in the larger community to advance the contributions of technical women.
